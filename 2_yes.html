Case study from National Library of Medicine on E-cigarette or Vaping Product
Use-Associated Lung Injury (EVALI) showed these alarming statistics [7]
High Prevalence Among Teens: The study diagnosed
twenty-five patients with EVALI, with a mean age of just
16.5 years, highlighting that this severe condition
predominantly affects teenagers.

Mental Health Issues: Alarmingly, the most common
comorbidity among these patients was a mental health
condition. Ten patients had anxiety and/or depression,
which is over 40% of the sample, demonstrating a strong
link between vaping and mental health issues.

Severe Respiratory Symptoms: Over half of the patients
presented with fever and gastrointestinal symptoms, with
dyspnea (difficulty breathing) being the most common
respiratory complaint (48%). This shows the severe impact
of vaping on respiratory health.

Substance Use and Detection: A staggering 96% of the
patients who underwent urine drug screens tested positive
for THC, suggesting a high prevalence of marijuana vaping
among the affected individuals. Additionally, most patients
(52%) denied using other forms of nicotine or marijuana
inhalation, highlighting vaping as the primary method of
substance use.

Significant Hospitalization Rates: Out of the twenty-five
patients, twenty-four required hospitalization, with an average
stay of 7.8 days. The severity of the condition is further
including intubation and ECMO for some patients.
