Resources available to you
How to start? Here are five points you can follow to start your journey!
1. Set a Quit Date and Make a Plan:
In two weeks, set a date to quit vaping. Describe your reasons for
quitting and create a plan. Incorporate craving and withdrawal
management.

2. Avoid Vaping Triggers:
Identify specific situations and environments where you usually vape.
For instance, if you vape while driving, change your routine by
listening to audiobooks or podcasts to keep your mind occupied.

3. Create a Support Network:
Ask a trusted friend or family member to be your “quit buddy.”
Regularly check in with them, especially during tough moments,
to share your progress and get encouragement. You can also join
online forums or social media groups dedicated to quitting vaping.

4. Substitute Hand-to-Mouth Habit:
Replace the physical habit of vaping with another activity that keeps
your hands busy. Try activities like knitting, drawing, or using a stress
ball. Having physical activity can help distract you from the urge to vape.

5. Track Your Progress:
Use a quitting app such as Smokeless or QuitSure to monitor your
progress. These apps can track how many days you’ve been vape-free,
how much money you’ve saved, and the health benefits you’re
experiencing. Seeing tangible progress can boost your motivation.

1. Health Canada - Quit Vaping:
This page provides comprehensive information on the steps to quit
vaping, including developing a quit plan, managing withdrawal symptoms,
and finding support. It also offers tools like the quit calculator and the
quitline number for personalized support. To ask for help, you can call the
Canadian Tobacco and Vaping Quitline at 1-866-366-3667.

https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/smoking-tobacco/
vaping/quit-vaping.html


2. QuitNow:
QuitNow is a free program by the BC Lung Association that provides
resources and support for quitting smoking and vaping. It includes a
variety of tools such as personalized quit plans, live chat with quit
coaches, and community forums. To ask for help, you can sign up on
their website or call their quitline at 1-877-455-2233.

https://www.quitnow.ca/


3. Smokers’ Helpline:
Offered by the Canadian Cancer Society, this helpline provides support
for quitting smoking and vaping through phone, text, and online chat
services. They also offer a variety of resources including a quit plan and
motivational support. To ask for help, you can call their helpline at
1-877-513-5333 or text iQUIT to 123456.

https://www.ontario.ca/page/support-quit-smoking
Thank you!
